Context

A regulated-industry case management platform that replaces a paper-and-spreadsheet workflow for ~200 caseworkers across one state agency.

Background

The agency manages safeguarding cases for vulnerable clients. The current workflow is partly paper (intake forms, consent capture) and partly spreadsheet (case tracking, supervisor allocations). State-level audits in 2024 and 2025 flagged inconsistent record-keeping and gaps in audit trails. The agency was given a 24-month window to move to a digital case management platform that meets the new safeguarding-standard data handling rules.

A commercial off-the-shelf vendor was evaluated in early 2025 and rejected on cost (annual licensing exceeded the agency's IT budget by ~3x) and integration friction (no path to the agency's existing finance system). The agency leadership chose to commission a custom platform with a focused feature scope.

The agency's CTO selected an external delivery partner in March 2026 after a procurement process. This document is the start of the partner's KB for the engagement.

Sponsor and stakeholders

Sponsor

External engagement. Hannah Reilly, CTO of the agency, is the executive sponsor and budget holder. Her primary interest is delivering a platform that passes the next state audit (Q2 2027) without escalation. She has explicit authority to deprioritise scope but not to extend the timeline.

Other stakeholders

  • Marcus Tan, Director of Safeguarding - operational owner of the new platform. Cares about caseworker adoption and not increasing time-on-task during transition. Will block features that complicate the day-to-day.
  • Caseworker representative committee (5 members) - voice of the day-to-day users. Care about workflow speed, mobile usability on agency tablets, and minimising training overhead.
  • Supervisor cohort (12 supervisors) - care about caseload visibility and reassignment workflows. Frustrated by current spreadsheet allocation; this is a strong supporter group.
  • State audit oversight body - external regulator. Cares about audit-trail completeness and data residency. Will conduct annual audits and ad-hoc spot checks.
  • Agency security team - cares about identity provider integration, MFA on supervisor accounts, and pen-test cadence.
  • Agency finance - cares only about the integration with their existing reconciliation system. Wants read-only access; will block any write integration in v1.
  • Agency communications - cares about messaging during transition; not a technical stakeholder but holds veto on rollout timing.

Business motivation

The state audit window closes Q2 2027. A failed audit triggers state-level intervention with downstream funding implications and potential leadership change. Successfully delivering the platform passes the audit and unlocks an additional state grant tied to digital transformation milestones (estimated $1.2M over three years). The platform itself is expected to reduce caseworker time-on-task by ~30% based on caseworker self-reports during discovery, recovering roughly 40 FTE-hours per week across the team.

Adjacent systems and prior art

Existing systems

  • Agency identity provider (SAML 2.0 SSO) - the platform integrates as a service provider; no separate user management.
  • Finance reconciliation system - read-only API integration for billing line-up. The integration contract is fixed; we adapt to it.
  • Existing intranet portal - users will be linked to the new platform from the intranet's "tools" panel.
  • Document storage (agency-managed Azure Files) - case attachments live here; the platform stores references, not blobs.

Prior attempts

  • Vendor evaluation, Q1 2025 - shortlist of three vendors evaluated against MoSCoW requirements list. All three rejected: two on price, one on data-residency (US-hosted, fails the state's residency rules).
  • Internal "case tracker" spreadsheet (since 2018) - the platform replaces this. Lessons: supervisors rely heavily on the per-region pivot view; replicate it.
  • Paper intake archive - the historical record is paper. Migration of records older than 2024 is explicitly out of scope (see Requirements).

External constraints

  • State data-residency rules. All data at rest and in transit must remain within the state's jurisdiction. Cloud region selection is constrained to a small set of approved Azure regions.
  • Audit window. Pilot ready by Q3 2026, full rollout by end of Q1 2027, audit-readiness by Q2 2027. Non-negotiable - tied to state oversight calendar.
  • Procurement framework. The engagement falls under a state-government procurement framework with mandatory monthly reporting on milestones, spend, and risk.
  • Agency change-management policy. All UI changes affecting caseworkers must be communicated 14 days before deploy via the agency's internal comms team. Constrains release cadence in the rollout phase.
  • Election cycle. The state minister responsible for the agency rotates every 4 years; the next rotation is mid-2027. Project must be operational and stable before that handover or risk strategic reset.
